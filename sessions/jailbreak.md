## Overview

**Time:** Late night, Day 2. The twin suns have set.
**Deadline:** The crew must free Henry and reach Old Arno before **Second Dawn (Day 3)**. Arno leaves with or without them.
**Tone:** Tense, heist-style. Think *Old West jailbreak* — the crew needs to infiltrate a small-town police station, spring their Wookiee, and get out clean. Or loud. Their call.

**What the players know:**
- Henry was arrested by the Mos Eisley militia after his berserker rage in the streets
- Old Arno departs at first light — no Henry means no muscle in the Wastes
- The Mandalorian and most bounty hunters have already left for the desert — the city is quieter than usual
- Henry's gear (bowcaster, etc.) was confiscated

**What the players don't know:**
- The police station is lightly staffed overnight — skeleton crew
- Lt. Harburik (Chief of Police) has gone home for the night
- Henry is in one of four basement holding cells
- The station has multiple entry points they can exploit

## The Mos Eisley Police Station
*Source: WEG40069, Location 26, pp. 72–73*

### Background

Prefect Talmont recently expanded the police force to 20 full-time officers (up from a part-time militia and half a dozen stormtroopers). The larger force required a new building in the newer sections of the city. The militia-turned-police are laid-back about crime and corruption — this is Mos Eisley, after all.

### Overnight Staffing (Skeleton Crew)

| Post | Personnel | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Room 1 — Desk Clerk | 1 officer | Monitors all cells, entrances, roof, basement, and corridors via security screens |
| Room 12 — Duty Station | 1 officer | Elevated post overlooking all 4 cells. **Sole access point to the cells.** |
| Room 14 — Speeder Parking | 1 officer | On patrol rotation, may be out on rounds. Returns every 30–45 min. |
| Rooftop | Nobody | Landing pad unmanned at night |
| Perimeter Alley (Room 11) | Nobody | Curved hallway below main floor — dark and empty |

**Total on-site:** 2–3 officers overnight. During the day there would be 8–10.

### Lt. Harburik — Chief of Police
*Not present overnight — home sleeping. Carries a comlink at all times. If alerted, arrives in 10–15 minutes.*

Corner office (Room 5). Hands-on leader, prefers fieldwork to desk duty. Rarely behind his desk. If he shows up during the jailbreak, the situation escalates significantly.

### Cell Security (Room 13)

Henry is in one of the four cells. Each cell has:

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| **Front wall** (facing Duty Station) | One-way transparisteel mirrored window. The duty officer can see in; prisoner sees a mirror. **Strength 6D.** |
| **Rear wall** | Iron bars. **Strength 4D.** |
| **Cell lock** | **Easy *security*** to pick — but **triggers an alarm** at both the Duty Station and Desk Clerk monitors. |
| **Other** | Bunk, reading lamps, restroom facilities, mirror. |

**Key insight for players:** The iron bars (Strength 4D) on the back wall are significantly weaker than the transparisteel windows (Strength 6D) on the front. A Wookiee with 6D Strength might be able to bend them from inside if given enough time and motivation.

| **STRENGTH** | 4D |
| **All others** | 2D |

**Morale:** These are local cops, not stormtroopers. They're underpaid, bored, and not looking to die for a Wookiee in a holding cell. If outgunned or surprised, they'll surrender or flee and call for backup rather than fight to the death.

**Backup response:** If an alarm is triggered, 2D6 minutes for 4 additional officers to arrive. Lt. Harburik arrives 10–15 minutes after that.

## Possible Approaches

These are not prescriptive — the players will come up with their own plan. These are just approaches to have prepped.

### 1. The Con — "We're Here for the Wookiee"
**Approach:** Walk in the front door. Bluff, bribe, or intimidate the desk clerk.

- **Bribe:** Mos Eisley cops are corrupt. 500 credits gets attention, 1,000 makes problems go away. Moderate *con* or *persuasion* to find the right price without raising suspicion.
- **Legal bluff:** Claim to be the Wookiee's legal representative. Dooble (protocol droid) could sell this. Moderate *bureaucracy* or *con*.
- **Intimidation:** Name-drop Jabba or another power. Difficult *intimidation* — cops don't scare easily but they know who runs this town.

**Complication:** Even if they talk their way past the desk clerk, they still need to get past the security doors (Room 10) and the duty officer (Room 12) to reach the cells.

### 2. The Sneak — Roof or Ramp
**Approach:** Enter via the unmanned rooftop landing pad or the basement speeder ramp.

- **Roof:** Climb up (Easy *climbing*), take the ladder (Room 15) down to speeder parking (Room 14). From there, navigate through the station to the cell block.
- **Ramp:** Enter through the sand trap cover on the speeder ramp (Room 16). Leads to the basement garage. Quieter, but still need to get upstairs and through security doors.
- **Sneak rolls:** Moderate *sneak* to move through the station undetected. The desk clerk's monitors are the biggest threat — if the crew doesn't disable or distract from them, they'll be spotted.

**Complication:** Both routes still require getting through the security doors (Room 10) and past the duty officer (Room 12).

### 3. The Distraction
**Approach:** Create a diversion outside the station to draw officers away, then infiltrate.

- **Fire, explosion, brawl:** Something loud enough to pull the desk clerk and/or duty officer outside. If only 2–3 officers are on-site, even one leaving creates a major opening.
- **Gune's specialty:** The man loves explosions. A controlled detonation a block away could empty the station.
- **Risk:** More noise = faster backup response. And Gune has a *reputation* for blowing things up.

### 4. The Brute Force
**Approach:** Kick in the door and shoot their way to the cells.

- Front door can be locked (Strength 6D). Security doors (Strength 6D). Iron bars on cells (Strength 4D).
- With only 2–3 officers, a direct assault would be quick — but loud. Backup arrives in 2D6 minutes.
- Henry could help from inside — a raging Wookiee vs. Strength 4D iron bars is a fair fight.

### 5. Henry Helps Himself
**Approach:** Signal Henry somehow (visitation room? comlink smuggled in?) and coordinate a breakout from both sides.

- Henry can attempt to bend the iron bars (Strength 4D) from inside. His STR is likely enough with effort, especially if enraged.
- The cell lock is Easy *security* — if anyone can get a tool to him, he can pick it (though it triggers the alarm).
- A combined inside-outside approach could be very effective.

## Complications & Escalation

### The Alarm
If the cell lock is picked or the security doors are forced, alarms trigger at both the Desk Clerk station and the Duty Station. Response:

| Time | Event |
|---|---|
| Immediate | Desk Clerk locks front door, calls for backup on comlink |
| 2D6 minutes | 4 additional officers arrive (2 from patrol, 2 from home) |
| 10–15 min after alarm | Lt. Harburik arrives, takes command |
| 20+ minutes | If situation escalates, stormtroopers from the Imperial garrison may respond |

### Other Prisoners
The other 3 cells may or may not hold prisoners. GM's choice:
- **Empty** — simplest option
- **A drunk** — comic relief, stumbles around getting in the way
- **A bounty hunter** — one of the hunters from the cantina fight, also arrested. Could be hostile, could want to team up to escape
- **A local** — grateful if freed, could become a minor ally or source of information

### Henry's State of Mind
Henry is scared, confused, and ashamed after his berserker episode. Gune's presence will calm him. Without Gune, Henry may be difficult to manage — not aggressive, but frozen with anxiety. Gune should be part of whatever group enters the cell block.

### The Property Room
Henry's gear is in Room 3. The crew should think to grab it — an unarmed Wookiee in the desert is still dangerous, but a Wookiee with a bowcaster is *terrifying*. The property room also has their confiscated weapons if any crew members were disarmed during the cantina fight.

## Rewards & Consequences

### Clean Getaway (No alarm, no violence)
- No heat from the militia — the overnight crew is embarrassed and covers it up
- Smooth departure with Old Arno at dawn
- Henry's arrest record quietly disappears (Mos Eisley cops have bigger problems)

### Messy but Successful (Alarm triggered, minor violence)
- The militia puts out a general alert — the crew is now wanted in Mos Eisley
- Old Arno is unimpressed but still guides them ("You sure know how to make friends")
- Returning to Mos Eisley later will be complicated

### Disaster (Prolonged firefight, officers killed)
- The crew becomes priority targets for the militia AND any remaining Imperial presence
- Possible stormtrooper pursuit into the Wastes
- Lt. Harburik takes it personally — he'll be a recurring problem
- Gune blowing up a *third* thing becomes legendary in Imperial propaganda

## Scene Pacing

| Beat | What Happens |
|---|---|
| **Setup** | The crew regroups at the *Centennial Duck* or the inn. They know Henry is in jail and dawn is coming. Let them plan. |
| **Reconnaissance** | If they scout the station first, describe the building exterior — new construction, flat roof with landing pad, speeder ramp on the side, sand-colored walls, light spilling from the front entrance. A single patrol scooter outside. |
| **Infiltration** | Whatever plan they choose. Play up the tension — quiet corridors, humming monitors, the distant sound of the desert wind. |
| **The Cell** | They find Henry sitting on his bunk, knees to his chest. He looks up. If Gune is there, he whimpers softly. He's not the raging monster from the cantina — he's a scared kid. |
| **Escape** | Get out with Henry and his gear. If things went loud, this is a chase scene. If quiet, they slip into the Mos Eisley night. |
| **Dawn** | Old Arno is waiting at the edge of town with a beat-up landspeeder. He looks at Henry, looks at the crew, and says nothing. They head into the Wastes. Transition to Episode 5. |
